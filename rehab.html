Rehabilitation of solar home systems

Cerritos de los Morreños-Golf of Guayaquil-Ecuador.

This project began in 2018, it consists of two phases per year, I joined the project in early 2019.

The group that led the logistics of the project consisted of 6 people, for this phase I could begin to carry out activities of coordination of the group that would attend, purchase of equipment for the solar system and communication with the people of the community, during this phase we mobilized for a whole week to the community to rehabilitate 20 solar home systems. Before starting this project, the inhabitants had a generator that provided them with electricity from 5 pm to 11 pm, with a value of $1 per day.

For the second phase I was leading the team, so I was involved in the design of the solar system, choice of equipment, logistics work and encourage the team to meet the goal of rehabilitating 25 solar home systems in the next trip to the community, and so we did during early 2020, where in addition to the installation of solar panels, we trained the people of the community to understand the systems and know-how to maintain them. The pandemic represented a new challenge because although we have not been able to travel again, the project has not stopped, and currently, the idea of creating a Smart grid network in the community is being raised.

IEEE was able to recognize the problems faced by this community and hand in hand with the University of Villanova and my university (ESPOL), decided to begin studies to provide the community with another source of energy, after an arduous study it was determined that solar energy would be the ideal solution for them.

Lessons learned:

  • 1. Leadership
  • 2.Skills communication
  • 3.Solar home systems
  • 4.Skills to design SROI (Social return of investment)
